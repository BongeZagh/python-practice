Daily chart update 8/6/11

I am not making a trade recommendation. This is simply my opinion about the chart over the next few weeks.

The chart on the right is the daily chart, and the one of the left is the weekly chart.

The weekly chart fell below the TL drawn from the bull L of March 2009 and found support this week at the TL drawn as a parallel of the TCL. It also bounced off the TL drawn from 7/7/09. This sharp selloff might turn out to be a simple sell vacuum down to a support level where strong bulls have been waiting, planning to buy relentless once there. The bear spike is so strong that even if the mkt rallies for a 10 bars or so, there is a 60% chance that it tests this level before too long (20 - 30 bars maybe). The mkt broke far below the HST and tested into the Lr TR, which is always support. There is about a 60% chance of a BT of the bottom of the upper TR (the HST) in the 1250 area and the weekly MA over the next few weeks.

The daily chart had three consec SXs 2 5 8, and 6 was a pause after a strong 2 bar SX to 5. 6 has a bull body and a big tail, so it is a PB, and it might end up being a one bar FF, as often happens when there are 3 consec SXs where the 2nd is very large and the 3rd is huge. The odds are maybe 70% that it tests above the 7 H within a 10 bars. the correction up should have at least 2 legs, last about 10 or more bars, and test the MA. It might even test the top of the 2nd SX 4. When a bear spike that has gone on for about 5-10 bars has a big tail, it usually tests down for one more bar, so the mkt might fall below the 8 L in the next couple of days (maybe on FOMC Tues). I do not know if the bottom of the next few weeks is the bar 8 L or if the mkt will go below it for a bar or two, but I believe that the odds are 70% that the mkt will rally above 7 within the next 10 bars. After that, anything can happen.

Options are safer than stocks and futures for traders holding overnight in an especially dangerous mkt (one where L P bear events can be huge). One big problem with options is that if the mkt rallies as is likely, the option premium will fall dramatically, causing the value of the options to shrink quickly. This means that a person can B a call and be correct about a big rally, but his profit might end up being much less than he expected due to the fear factor within the premium disappearing.

The selloff has been so severe that there is a 30% chance that the bear spike grows for many more bars and that the market drops 20% or more beyond where it is now before there is any PB. In a bear, if the mkt is in a bear, L P downside surprises can be huge, as they have been on the 5 min chart on several days over the past couple of weeks. However, this means that there is a 70% that it won't collapse near term.

I still believe that the mkt has a 50% chance of falling below the 2009 within the next few years. It has been in a TR on the monthly chart since about 1998 and the odds are that the BO attempt below will fail and lead to a test of the top of the range over the following years.

The percentages that I gave above are my opinion based on experience and may be very far the reality. Every tick gives new information and my opinion can change and even reverse suddenly. My job is to follow the mkt, and sometimes I suddenly realize that the mkt is doing the opposite of what I thought it was doing. When I do, I don¬ít question it. I simply look to trade in the other direction.
